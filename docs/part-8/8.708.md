# 8.708 Shipping

(a) Delivery is accomplished when a shipment  is placed aboard the vehicle of the initial carrier. The

time of delivery is the date shipment  is released to and accepted by the initial carrier.

(b) Shipment  is normally under Government  bills of lading. However, for small orders, ordering

offices  may specify other shipment  methods.

(c) When shipments  are under Government  bills of lading, the bills of lading may accompany orders

or be otherwise furnished promptly. Failure of an ordering office  to furnish bills of lading or to

designate a method of transportation may result in an excusable delay in delivery.

(d) AbilityOne participating nonprofit agencies shall  include transportation costs for small shipments

paid by the nonprofit agencies as an item on the invoice . The ordering office  shall  reimburse the

nonprofit agencies for these costs.
