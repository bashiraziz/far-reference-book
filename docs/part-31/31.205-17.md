# 31.205-17 Idle facilities and idle capacity costs

(a) Definitions . As used in this subsection-

Costs  of idle facilities  or idle capacity  means costs  such as maintenance, repair, housing, rent, and

other related costs ; e.g., property taxes, insurance , and depreciation .

Facilities  means plant or any portion thereof (including land integral to the operation), equipment,

individually or collectively, or any other tangible capital asset , wherever located, and whether owned

or leased by the contractor.

Idle capacity  means the unused capacity of partially used facilities . It is the difference between that

which a facility could achieve under 100 percent operating time on a one-shift basis, less operating

interruptions resulting from time lost for repairs, setups, unsatisfactory materials, and other normal

delays, and the extent to which the facility was actually used to meet demands during the accounting

period. A multiple-shift basis may be used in the calculation instead of a one-shift basis if it can be

shown that this amount of usage could normally be expected for the type of facility involved.
